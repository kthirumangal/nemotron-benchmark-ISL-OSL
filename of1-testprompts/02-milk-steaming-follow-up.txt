### Key Features
- PID Temperature Control: A PID (Proportional-Integral-Derivative) controller precisely regulates the boiler temperature, maintaining accuracy within 0.5 degrees Celsius. This eliminates the temperature surfing required with older thermostat-based machines and ensures shot-to-shot consistency. PID control is considered essential for serious espresso brewing.
- Pressure Profiling: Pressure profiling allows you to vary the extraction pressure throughout the shot, rather than maintaining a flat 9 bars. By controlling the pressure curve, you can emphasize different flavor characteristics: a gentle ramp-up reduces channeling, while a declining profile can enhance sweetness and body. This is the frontier of espresso extraction.
- Dual Boiler System: A dual boiler system uses separate boilers for brewing and steaming, each with independent temperature control. This means you can brew espresso and steam milk simultaneously without any temperature compromise. The brew boiler stays at the optimal extraction temperature while the steam boiler runs hotter for powerful, dry steam.
- Flat Burr Grinder: Flat burrs consist of two parallel disc-shaped burrs that slice coffee beans into highly uniform particles. The result is a cleaner, brighter cup with more clarity and definition in the flavor. Flat burrs are favored by those who want to taste the distinct origin character of their coffee with precision and transparency.
- Conical Burr Grinder: Conical burrs use a cone-shaped inner burr that fits inside a ring-shaped outer burr. The geometry produces a bimodal particle distribution that many people find creates a sweeter, fuller-bodied cup with more perceived richness. Conical burrs tend to run cooler and quieter than flat burrs.
